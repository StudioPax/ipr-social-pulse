# Welcome to Meridian

Meridian is an AI-powered social media intelligence platform that helps organizations understand what's working in their communications, surface what's being missed, and plan what comes next.

It was built for research institutions, policy organizations, and mission-driven teams who need more than engagement metrics — you need to know whether your content is advancing your mission.

---

## What Meridian Does

Meridian works in four stages. Each one builds on the previous:

**Collect** — Meridian pulls posts from your social media accounts into a single, unified content library. No more toggling between platforms or exporting CSVs. You choose the date range, hit collect, and everything is normalized into one view.

> Currently supported: Bluesky, LinkedIn. X/Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram are in development.

**Analyze** — This is where the AI comes in. Meridian sends your collected posts to an AI model (Claude or Gemini — your choice) which reads each one and classifies it across several dimensions: topic alignment with your organization's focus areas, sentiment, audience fit, performance tier, and strategic communications quality. Every classification comes with a confidence score and a written rationale, so you can see *why* the AI made each call.

**Plan** — When new research or initiatives launch, Meridian's campaign workspace helps you plan multi-channel content. You bring the context (your research summary, key talking points, supporting materials), and the AI generates audience-specific narratives, channel-adapted drafts, and a strategic framework you can refine and publish from.

**Track** — Once content is live, Meridian tracks who's engaging, which posts are landing, and where the gaps are. The dashboard gives you a clear picture at any moment.

> The Outreach module (amplifier tracking, influencer tiering) is in development.

## The Workflow

Here's what using Meridian looks like in practice:

### 1. Connect your accounts

In Settings, add your social platform credentials. Meridian connects through official APIs — it never scrapes or accesses anything outside your public posts.

### 2. Collect your content

From the Content page, run a collection for your desired date range. Meridian pulls in posts, deduplicates automatically, and stores everything in your content library. You can also import content from files if you've extracted posts manually.

### 3. Run AI analysis

Select an AI model and click analyze. Meridian processes your posts in batches, and a real-time log shows you exactly what's happening. When it's done, every post in your library has structured intelligence attached to it — not just numbers, but strategic context.

### 4. Review and act

Use filters and sorting to slice your content by topic, performance tier, sentiment, or audience. Expand any post to see the full analysis detail. Meridian suggests a next action for each post:

| Action | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Amplify** | This is already working. Boost it, cross-post it, share it wider. |
| **Template** | The format is effective. Study what works here and apply it elsewhere. |
| **Promote** | Important content that deserves more visibility. Invest in getting it seen. |
| **Diagnose** | Something isn't connecting. Investigate timing, framing, or audience fit. |

### 5. Plan campaigns

When you have a major piece of content to roll out, create a campaign. Upload your source materials, add your notes, and let the AI generate a multi-channel strategy. You review, refine, and own every decision — the AI gives you a strong starting point.

### 6. Monitor from the dashboard

The dashboard surfaces trends, top performers, topic distribution, and strategic gaps. It's designed to be shareable with leadership — a clear, visual answer to "how are we doing?"

## How the AI Works

A few things worth knowing:

**You choose the model.** Meridian supports Claude (by Anthropic) and Gemini (by Google). Both produce the same structured output. Your API keys are stored securely and never exposed in the browser.

**The AI reads, it doesn't post.** It analyzes your content and generates drafts, but it never publishes, sends, or modifies anything on your behalf.

**Every output is a recommendation.** Pillar tags, sentiment scores, tier placements, campaign drafts — they're all starting points for your judgment, not final decisions.

**Your data stays yours.** Posts are processed through the AI's API and are not used for model training. Your content library is isolated to your account.

---

## Key Concepts

**Focus areas** — Your organization's core topic areas (e.g., policy pillars, research themes). Meridian's AI tags every post to one of your defined focus areas so you can see whether your content presence is balanced.

**Performance tiers** — Every post is placed on a 2x2 matrix of engagement vs. mission relevance:
- **T1** — High engagement, high relevance. The gold standard.
- **T2** — High engagement, moderate relevance. Good reach, less mission-aligned.
- **T3** — Low engagement, high relevance. Your biggest opportunity — important work that needs more visibility.
- **T4** — Low engagement, low relevance. Worth investigating.

**Strategic framing score** — Meridian evaluates every post against the FrameWorks Institute's strategic communications methodology. This gives you a concrete quality metric grounded in research, not just engagement.

**Campaigns** — Project-style workspaces for planning how to communicate a specific piece of research or initiative across multiple channels and audiences.

## Getting Started

Your Meridian instance comes pre-configured with your organization's focus areas, audience segments, and analysis criteria. To get up and running:

1. **Add your AI API key** in Settings (Claude or Gemini)
2. **Connect your social accounts** in Settings
3. **Run your first collection** from the Content page
4. **Analyze** to see your content library come alive with intelligence
5. **Explore the dashboard** to see the big picture
